As a subject, mathematics is surprisingly hard to pin down with one catch-all definition.


There is no such thing as “the most interesting aspect of maths”. Yes, numbers are cool, but so are geometry, topology, computer science, cryptography. Mathematics is not simply stuff to do with numbers, but a huge range of topics. So, the idea behind this game is that one can always explore the maths that they most enjoy.

The game is simple enough: you click on the heart sign to choose between 2 cards, and the card not chosen will be replaced. A card has 2 sides, and you can click on the pic to flip to the backside and read more info. Each card represents a mathematical topic with a brief intro on the first side, and a side story or application on the second side.

Still not sure how the game works? Play a demo game here with some animal pictures.


P/s: The content of the cards are subject to my own research and preferences, and therefore not representative of the complexities of the Mathematics field. However, I hope it can be a good start to learning something new for you! The main references for my topic research are a book called “The Maths Book: Big Ideas Simply Explained”, plenty of Numberphile and Tom’s videos, and the Facebook Group “Mathematical Mathematics Memes”
